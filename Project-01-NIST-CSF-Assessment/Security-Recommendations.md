## Purpose

This document provides recommendations based on the findings documented throughout the Enterprise Cybersecurity Assessment.

The recommendations are intended to improve the organization's cybersecurity posture by addressing identified risks, strengthening existing controls, and supporting continued alignment with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0.

Recommendations have been prioritized according to their potential impact on organizational risk and the estimated effort required for implementation.

-----

## Recommendation Priorities

Recommendations are categorized using the following priority levels.

| Priority | Description                                                                           |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| High     | Addresses significant organizational risk or foundational cybersecurity capabilities. |
| Medium   | Improves security maturity and operational consistency.                               |
| Low      | Enhances existing capabilities or supports long-term program maturity.                |

## Detailed Recommendations

### REC-001

Recommendation

Formalize the organization's cybersecurity governance program.

Reason

Although security responsibilities exist, governance activities should be more structured to improve oversight, accountability, and strategic planning.

Expected Benefits

* Improved executive visibility
* Better alignment between business and cybersecurity objectives
* More consistent decision-making
* Clear ownership of cybersecurity initiatives

Related Risk

R-001

Estimated Implementation Effort

Medium

-----

### REC-002

Recommendation

Maintain an enterprise cybersecurity risk register that is reviewed on a recurring schedule.

Reason

A centralized risk register provides leadership with a clear understanding of organizational cyber risk and helps prioritize remediation efforts.

Expected Benefits

* Improved risk visibility
* Better remediation tracking
* More consistent risk management
* Better support for future assessments

Related Risk

R-002

Estimated Implementation Effort

Low

-----

### REC-003

Recommendation

Implement a formal security policy management process.

Reason

Policies should remain accurate, current, and consistently reviewed throughout their lifecycle.

Expected Benefits

* Improved policy consistency
* Clear document ownership
* Better compliance support
* Reduced confusion for employees

Related Risk

R-003

Estimated Implementation Effort

Medium

-----

### REC-004

Recommendation

Develop a standardized third-party risk management process.

Reason

Third-party vendors can introduce cybersecurity risks that should be evaluated throughout the vendor relationship.

Expected Benefits

* Improved vendor oversight
* Better understanding of supply chain risk
* More consistent vendor assessments
* Reduced organizational exposure

Related Risk

R-004

Estimated Implementation Effort

Medium

-----

### REC-005

Recommendation

Develop executive cybersecurity reporting using measurable security metrics.

Reason

Leadership should receive consistent information regarding cybersecurity performance and organizational risk.

Expected Benefits

* Better executive decision-making
* Increased visibility into cybersecurity trends
* Improved communication
* Support for strategic planning

Related Risk

R-005

Estimated Implementation Effort

Medium

-----

### REC-006

Recommendation

Conduct annual incident response tabletop exercises.

Reason

Testing response procedures helps validate plans before an actual security incident occurs.

Expected Benefits

* Improved coordination
* Better organizational preparedness
* Identification of procedural gaps
* Continuous improvement of response capabilities

Related Risk

R-006

Estimated Implementation Effort

Low

-----

### REC-007

Recommendation

Implement a Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M) process for vulnerability remediation.

Reason

A formal remediation tracking process improves accountability and ensures that identified vulnerabilities are addressed within expected timeframes.

Expected Benefits

* Improved remediation tracking
* Defined ownership
* Increased accountability
* Better reporting of remediation progress

Related Risk

R-007

Estimated Implementation Effort

Medium

-----

### REC-008

Recommendation

Perform quarterly user access reviews.

Reason

Regular access reviews help ensure that users maintain only the permissions necessary to perform their job responsibilities.

Expected Benefits

* Improved least privilege
* Reduced insider risk
* Better access governance
* More accurate user permissions

Related Risk

R-008

Estimated Implementation Effort

Low

-----

### REC-009

Recommendation

Develop measurable security awareness metrics.

Reason

Training effectiveness should be evaluated using objective performance data rather than completion rates alone.

Expected Benefits

* Improved employee awareness
* Better identification of training needs
* Measurable program improvement
* Increased resilience against phishing attacks

Related Risk

R-009

Estimated Implementation Effort

Low

-----

### REC-010

Recommendation

Perform annual disaster recovery exercises.

Reason

Recovery procedures should be validated periodically to confirm that critical systems can be restored within established recovery objectives.

Expected Benefits

* Increased confidence in recovery procedures
* Validation of Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs)
* Validation of Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs)
* Improved organizational resilience

Related Risk

R-010

Estimated Implementation Effort

Medium

## Expected Outcomes

Successful implementation of these recommendations is expected to:

* Improve alignment with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.
* Reduce organizational cybersecurity risk.
* Strengthen governance and accountability.
* Improve visibility into organizational security posture.
* Support continuous improvement of the cybersecurity program.
* Provide a stronger foundation for future compliance initiatives.

-----

## Conclusion

The recommendations contained in this document represent practical, risk-based improvements intended to strengthen the cybersecurity posture of Northwind Health Solutions.

The recommendations are prioritized according to organizational risk and implementation effort, allowing leadership to address foundational governance activities first while establishing a roadmap for continued cybersecurity maturity. Future portfolio projects will demonstrate how several of these recommendations are implemented through updated policies, risk management practices, access governance, vulnerability management, and security reporting.
