## Purpose

The Enterprise Risk Register serves as the central repository for documenting cybersecurity risks identified during the Enterprise Cybersecurity Assessment.

The register enables Northwind Health Solutions to consistently identify, evaluate, prioritize, monitor, and remediate cybersecurity risks affecting business operations, information systems, and protected health information (PHI).

The register also provides leadership with a consolidated view of organizational cyber risk to support informed decision-making and risk-based resource allocation.

-----

## Risk Rating Methodology

Each risk is evaluated using qualitative assessments of likelihood and business impact.

### Likelihood Ratings

| Rating | Description                                                               |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Low    | Unlikely to occur under normal operating conditions.                      |
| Medium | Could reasonably occur if existing controls fail or circumstances change. |
| High   | Expected to occur without additional mitigating controls.                 |

### Impact Ratings

| Rating | Description                                                                                      |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Low    | Limited operational disruption with minimal financial or regulatory impact.                      |
| Medium | Noticeable disruption requiring management attention and moderate recovery effort.               |
| High   | Significant operational disruption, regulatory exposure, financial loss, or reputational damage. |

### Overall Risk Priority

Overall risk priority is determined by evaluating both likelihood and business impact while considering existing mitigating controls.

## Risk Details

### R-001

Risk Category

Cybersecurity Governance

Description

Governance processes have developed organically and lack standardized documentation defining governance structure, reporting expectations, review cycles, and executive oversight.

Potential Business Impact

* Inconsistent security decision-making.
* Reduced executive visibility into organizational risk.
* Delayed remediation activities.
* Difficulty demonstrating governance maturity.

Existing Controls

* Executive sponsorship.
* Annual security planning.
* Departmental management oversight.

Recommended Treatment

Develop a formal cybersecurity governance program including governance documentation, recurring leadership reviews, performance metrics, and accountability reporting.

Residual Risk

Medium

Target Completion

Quarter 2 2027

### R-002

Risk Category

Enterprise Risk Management

Description

Cybersecurity risks are identified during projects and assessments but are not maintained within a centralized enterprise risk management process.

Potential Business Impact

* Inconsistent prioritization.
* Duplicate remediation efforts.
* Limited visibility into enterprise cyber risk.

Existing Controls

* Individual project assessments.
* Periodic management discussions.

Recommended Treatment

Develop and maintain a centralized Enterprise Risk Register with defined ownership, review schedules, mitigation tracking, and residual risk assessments.

Residual Risk

Low

Target Completion

Quarter 1 2027

Low

### R-007

Risk Category

Vulnerability Management

Description

Critical vulnerabilities are identified through routine scanning; however, remediation activities are not consistently monitored using formal tracking processes.

Potential Business Impact

* Increased exposure to known vulnerabilities.
* Missed remediation deadlines.
* Difficulty measuring remediation performance.

Existing Controls

* Monthly vulnerability scans.
* Patch management process.

Recommended Treatment

Implement a centralized Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M) to monitor remediation progress, assign ownership, establish due dates, and report remediation status.

Residual Risk

Medium

Target Completion

Quarter 1 2027

### R-008

Risk Category

Identity and Access Management

Description

Role-based access controls are implemented, but recurring access certification reviews are not consistently documented.

Potential Business Impact

* Excessive user permissions.
* Increased insider risk.
* Reduced accountability.

Existing Controls

* Active Directory.
* Microsoft Entra ID.
* Multi-factor authentication.

Recommended Treatment

Establish quarterly access certification reviews with documented approvals and remediation of inappropriate permissions.

Residual Risk

Low

Target Completion

Quarter 2 2027

### R-009

Risk Category

Security Awareness

Description

Employee security awareness training is conducted annually, but the effectiveness of the program is not measured using performance metrics.

Potential Business Impact

* Limited visibility into user risk.
* Reduced ability to improve awareness initiatives.

Existing Controls

* Annual awareness training.

Recommended Treatment

Measure phishing susceptibility, training completion rates, repeat training participation, and long-term user improvement trends.

Residual Risk

Low

Target Completion

Quarter 3 2027

### R-010

Risk Category

Business Continuity

Description

Disaster recovery documentation exists, but recovery procedures have not been routinely validated through formal testing.

Potential Business Impact

* Delayed restoration of critical systems.
* Increased operational disruption.
* Failure to meet recovery objectives.

Existing Controls

* Backup procedures.
* Disaster Recovery Plan.

Recommended Treatment

Conduct annual disaster recovery exercises, document results, validate Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs), and incorporate lessons learned into recovery planning.

Residual Risk

Low

Target Completion

Quarter 4 2027

## Conclusion

The Enterprise Risk Register establishes a structured, repeatable process for managing cybersecurity risk across Northwind Health Solutions.

The risks identified within this register will be reviewed periodically as remediation activities progress and organizational maturity improves. Future portfolio projects—including the Security Policy Library, Vulnerability Assessment, Identity and Access Review, Security Metrics Dashboard, and Implementation Roadmap—will directly support the treatment of the risks documented in this register.
