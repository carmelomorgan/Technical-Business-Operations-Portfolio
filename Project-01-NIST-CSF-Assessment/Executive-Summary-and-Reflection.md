## Purpose

This Executive Summary provides a high-level overview of the Enterprise Cybersecurity Assessment conducted for Northwind Health Solutions. It summarizes the assessment findings, highlights the most significant organizational risks, and outlines the recommended next steps for improving the organization's cybersecurity posture.

The goal of this assessment was to evaluate the current maturity of Northwind Health Solutions' cybersecurity program using the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 and to establish a practical roadmap for continued improvement.

-----

## Assessment Overview

The assessment evaluated the organization's cybersecurity program across the six functions defined by the NIST Cybersecurity Framework:

* Govern
* Identify
* Protect
* Detect
* Respond
* Recover

The review considered organizational documentation, security policies, risk management processes, technical controls, and operational procedures to develop an overall understanding of the organization's cybersecurity maturity.

Overall, Northwind Health Solutions demonstrates a developing cybersecurity program with many foundational technical controls already in place. Identity and access management, endpoint protection, vulnerability scanning, backup processes, and security awareness training provide a solid operational foundation. However, several governance and risk management processes require additional structure and documentation to support continued program maturity.

-----

## Key Findings

The assessment identified several strengths within the existing cybersecurity program.

* Core security controls have been implemented across the organization.
* Multi-factor authentication protects privileged accounts.
* Asset inventories and data classification standards have been established.
* Routine vulnerability scanning and endpoint protection are in place.
* Backup and disaster recovery procedures support business continuity.
* Leadership recognizes cybersecurity as an organizational priority.

The assessment also identified opportunities for improvement.

* Cybersecurity governance should be more formally documented.
* Enterprise risk management should be centralized through a formal risk register.
* Security policy management should include standardized ownership and review processes.
* Third-party risk management should be expanded throughout the vendor lifecycle.
* Executive cybersecurity reporting should include meaningful performance metrics.
* Incident response procedures should be validated through recurring tabletop exercises.
* Vulnerability remediation should be tracked through a formal POA&M process.

-----

## Overall Assessment

Based on the assessment, Northwind Health Solutions demonstrates an overall cybersecurity maturity that aligns with an organization transitioning from foundational security practices toward a more mature governance and risk management program.

The organization has established many of the technical safeguards expected of a healthcare provider, but continued investment in governance, documentation, measurement, and process maturity will improve consistency, reduce organizational risk, and better support long-term strategic objectives.

Rather than recommending large-scale technology changes, the assessment emphasizes strengthening existing processes and improving the effectiveness of current security controls.

-----

## Recommended Next Steps

The following activities are recommended as the organization's highest priorities.

1. Formalize cybersecurity governance processes.
2. Maintain an enterprise cybersecurity risk register.
3. Implement a centralized Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M).
4. Standardize security policy management.
5. Develop executive cybersecurity reporting.
6. Improve third-party risk management.
7. Conduct recurring incident response and disaster recovery exercises.

Successful completion of these initiatives will establish a stronger governance foundation while supporting continued alignment with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

## Project Reflection

### Design Decisions

This project was intentionally designed as an internal cybersecurity assessment rather than a consulting engagement. My objective was to demonstrate how an entry-level cybersecurity or GRC analyst might evaluate an organization's security posture using a structured and widely recognized framework.

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 was selected because it is commonly referenced across government agencies, healthcare organizations, and private industry. Using a well-established framework allowed the assessment to remain organized, repeatable, and aligned with industry best practices while remaining approachable for someone like myself who is just starting out in the field.

Northwind Health Solutions was intentionally portrayed as an organization with a developing cybersecurity program rather than one with either exceptionally poor or exceptionally mature security practices. This approach allowed for more realistic findings and recommendations to emerge while creating a logical foundation for the remaining projects in this portfolio. This project mainly focuses on governance, documentation, and risk management improvements because these are areas I have seen thus far commonly encountered by entry-level GRC positions and is consistent with the responsibilities described in many cybersecurity analyst positions as well.

Throughout the project, the emphasis remained on creating documentation that could reasonably be produced by a junior analyst, but used sound logic as opposed to experience. Where assumptions were necessary due to the fictional nature of the organization, I tried to make them conservatively and consistently to preserve realism.

-----

### Lessons Learned

Completing this project strengthened my understanding of how cybersecurity governance extends beyond mere technical controls. Prior to developing this assessment, I primarily viewed cybersecurity through the lens of technical safeguards. Working through the assessment process demonstrated that governance, documentation, communication, and risk management are equally important components of an effective cybersecurity program.

This project reinforced the relationship between organizational findings, identified risks, recommended improvements, and implementation planning. Rather than viewing these as separate documents, I learned how each artifact builds upon the previous one to create a structured and repeatable risk management process. I also gained a greater appreciation for the importance of documenting assumptions, supporting conclusions with evidence, and presenting recommendations in language that can be understood by both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Perhaps the most valuable lesson from this project was recognizing that effective GRC work is not about identifying every possible security issue, but about helping an organization understand its most significant risks and prioritizing practical improvements. Developing this assessment improved my technical writing, my overall understanding of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, and my confidence in explaining cybersecurity concepts in a clear and organized manner. It also established a strong foundation for the remaining projects in this portfolio, each of which will build upon the assessment completed here.
